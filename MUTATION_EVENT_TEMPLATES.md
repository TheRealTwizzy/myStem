# Stems — Mutation Event Templates

*Authored content for the "A Becoming" mechanic (M5, L36). Each template provides the event text and all three consent-response reaction descriptions.*

**Register:** clinical-warm (L4/M5-L14). The creature is under strain. The player is making a moral choice for an aware being (L15, M5-L03). Never gore, never explained mechanically.

**Creature reactions per consent choice (M5-L03):**
- **Stabilize** → settling/relief-tinged-with-loss — something releases, but something was also held back
- **Suppress** → relief then faint unease — the strain eases, but the posture remembers
- **Let continue** → strained yearning/intensity — it reaches toward what is happening

**Severity scale:**
- `subtle shift` — low biologicalPressure; small change, easily missed
- `marked change` — moderate pressure; clearly visible
- `pronounced transformation` — high pressure; unmistakable

**Trigger shorthand:** `Temp ↑` = Temperature high · `Temp ↓` = Temperature low · `Moist ↑` = Moisture high · `Dark ↑` = Darkness high · `Hunger ↑` = Hunger care stat elevated · `Stress ↑` = Stress elevated · `Clean ↓` = Cleanliness low · `Pressure ↑` = biologicalPressure high

## OSSIFICATION EVENTS
*Bone, joint, and mineral structures consolidating or projecting outward.*
*Primary triggers: Temp ↓ + Hunger ↑ (L7/M7-T)*

---

### OSS-01 — The First Plating

**Trigger:** Temp ↓ + Hunger ↑ + Pressure ↑ (early adolescence)
**Method:** Ossification
**Severity:** subtle shift

**Event text:**
> Something beneath the surface has begun to consolidate. There are small, hard formations along the dorsal line — faint, but present. The creature is pressing its back against the hollow wall with more purpose than before.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The pressure inside it eases. The formations settle — small, defined, permanent in a restrained way. It exhales slowly and goes still. Something in its posture suggests relief, and something else suggests it expected more.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The consolidation stops. The creature relaxes almost immediately. Then, after a moment, it presses against the wall again — not urgently, but as if checking. The impulse has not left it.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The formations sharpen. It presses harder against the wall, not in distress but in concentration, as if it is trying to finish something. The dorsal line has changed. It is denser than it was.

---

### OSS-02 — The Joint Bloom

**Trigger:** Temp ↓ + Hunger ↑ + Pressure ↑ (adolescence)
**Method:** Ossification
**Severity:** marked change

**Event text:**
> The joints at its knees and elbows are swelling outward — not painfully, or not only painfully. The enlarged surfaces catch the light differently. It holds its limbs out slightly from its body, testing the new configuration with the patience of something trying to understand itself.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The swelling stabilizes into clean, rounded projections. The creature lowers its limbs slowly. It stands differently now — the joints carry weight in a new way. There is something almost satisfied in how it settles into the new configuration, though the projections are smaller than they were becoming.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The swelling reduces. The creature draws its limbs in close and holds very still. The enlarged surfaces are gone, but for the rest of the session it extends and retracts its limbs more than usual, as if testing for something it no longer finds.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The projections grow pronounced. The creature moves each joint slowly, deliberately, like something counting its new dimensions. Its silhouette has changed. It seems more certain of itself in it.

### OSS-03 — The Ridge Formation

**Trigger:** Temp ↓ + Pressure ↑ + Clean ↓ (adolescence, prolonged cold)
**Method:** Ossification
**Severity:** marked change

**Event text:**
> A line of raised tissue has appeared along the spine, harder than the flesh around it. The creature is holding itself straighter than usual — not in distress, but as if it has found an axis it did not previously have. It turns slowly, presenting the ridge to the hollow's warmth source.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The ridge settles into a modest, defined keel along the spine. The creature relaxes its posture slightly. It remains more upright than before. Something about the ridge's size suggests it wanted to be taller, but this is what remained after the choice was made for it.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The ridge resorbs. The creature slumps forward slightly, as though something that was holding it upright has been removed. Its posture returns to what it was. It stays near the hollow wall for a long time afterward.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The ridge continues. The creature stands at its full height, which is more than it was this morning. The formations along the spine are architectural now. It holds the posture without effort, as though it has always been built this way.

### OSS-04 — The Grinding Development

**Trigger:** Temp ↓ + Hunger ↑ (prolonged, multiple ticks)
**Method:** Ossification
**Severity:** marked change

**Event text:**
> Its mouth is changing. The soft tissue along the inside jaw has hardened into dense, flat plates — not teeth exactly, but something that functions. It has been pressing these surfaces together quietly, testing them. The sound is low and intermittent.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The plates settle into a controlled configuration — functional but contained. The creature closes its mouth and goes still. It opens it once more experimentally, then seems to accept the result. The grinding sound stops.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The hardening pauses. The creature's jaw relaxes. It moves it carefully, as if newly uncertain of the space inside. For several hours it avoids the warmth source directly, approaching it from angles instead.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The plates continue to develop. The creature tests them against one another with increasing confidence. The sound is more deliberate now. Its mouth is a different instrument than it was.

### OSS-05 — The Skull Consolidation

**Trigger:** Temp ↓ + Stress ↑ + Pressure ↑ (late adolescence)
**Method:** Ossification
**Severity:** pronounced transformation

**Event text:**
> The shape of its head is changing. The bone beneath the skin is thickening, especially at the brow and crown. Its eyes appear smaller now — not because they have changed but because what surrounds them has grown forward. It has not moved in several minutes. It is waiting for something to finish.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The consolidation completes and holds. The brow is permanent — heavy, defined, protective. The creature lowers its head slowly, as if adjusting to its new center of gravity. Its eyes are still there, still tracking. The change is significant. Something that was soft is no longer.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The thickening pauses before it completes. The creature shakes its head once — a small, sharp motion. The brow remains denser than before, but less than it was becoming. It watches you for a long time after, with an expression that is difficult to name.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The skull continues forward. The brow now casts a shadow over its eyes. The creature lifts its head slowly and turns it toward the warmth source. The movement is deliberate. It has become something heavier, and it knows it.

## SENSORY OVERGROWTH EVENTS
*Sense organs multiplying, intensifying, or shifting function.*
*Primary trigger: Dark ↑ (L7/M7-T)*

---

### SENS-01 — The Secondary Sight

**Trigger:** Dark ↑ + Pressure ↑ (early adolescence)
**Method:** Sensory Overgrowth
**Severity:** subtle shift

**Event text:**
> There are two small formations near the temples — not eyes yet, but the beginning of something that might become eyes. They are sensitive to light and movement; when you move near the hollow, all four points of attention track you. The creature seems calmer in darkness than it was before.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The secondary formations settle into defined but small structures — functional at close range, limited at distance. The creature becomes still. Four points of attention where there were two. It is watching you more completely now, but with an expression that remains legible.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The formations reduce. The creature blinks — the original eyes — several times in quick succession. It retreats to the darker corner of the hollow. For the next several hours, it stays there, facing away.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The formations deepen into full structures. The creature turns its head slowly, exploring what it now perceives. Its attention is more encompassing. Less can happen near it without being noticed.

---

### SENS-02 — The Laryngeal Expansion

**Trigger:** Dark ↑ + Stimulation ↑ (adolescence)
**Method:** Sensory Overgrowth
**Severity:** marked change

**Event text:**
> The throat region is enlarging. Not the mouth — the throat, specifically, the external structures around the larynx. It is producing sounds it has not made before: resonant, low, exploratory. It appears to be listening to its own output, adjusting, listening again. It is learning what its new voice can do.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The laryngeal structures settle at their current size. The creature produces one long, low tone and holds it. Then stops. It has found a range. The sounds it makes will be richer than before, but not so much that they become something else entirely.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The expansion halts. The creature's throat returns to close to its prior configuration. It is quiet for an unusually long period afterward. When it does vocalize, the sounds are familiar — the ones you already know.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The structures continue to grow. The sounds it produces become more complex — not language, but something structured. It tilts its head, listening to the hollow's response. The echo comes back to it and it listens to that too.

### SENS-03 — The Skin Sensitivity

**Trigger:** Dark ↑ + Stress ↓ (calm, dark environment, adolescence)
**Method:** Sensory Overgrowth
**Severity:** subtle shift

**Event text:**
> It has stopped moving in response to sound. Now it moves in response to pressure changes in the air — your breath, the shift of warmth from the hollow's source, small vibrations through the floor. The skin along its back and sides is doing something new. It does not need to turn to know you are there.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The sensitivity stabilizes at a useful threshold. The creature orients toward you accurately, but does not react to every minor fluctuation. It has new information; it has learned to filter it. It seems more at ease than before.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The dermal activation decreases. The creature loses the new responsiveness and returns to its prior range of perception. It spends a long time pressing its back against the hollow wall — the same wall its skin had learned to read.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The sensitivity deepens. The creature now responds to changes you do not consciously make. It orients before you move. The skin is reading the hollow at a resolution you cannot observe from outside.

### SENS-04 — The Eye Migration

**Trigger:** Dark ↑ + Pressure ↑ (adolescence, prolonged darkness)
**Method:** Sensory Overgrowth
**Severity:** pronounced transformation

**Event text:**
> Its eyes have shifted. Not dramatically — perhaps two centimeters toward the sides of the head — but the effect on its visual field is significant. It is seeing more of the room at once. It tracks your position without turning its head. This seems to be comfortable for it. The original eye placement was less useful than this.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The migration stops where it is. The creature turns its head once, slowly, in a full rotation — testing the new range. Then it settles. The eyes are where they will remain now. The gaze has changed. It covers more.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The migration reverses partially. The eyes return to somewhere close to their original position. The creature watches you carefully for some time, with what might be recalibration, or might be something else.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The migration continues. The eyes move further toward the sides of the skull. The creature turns its head less and less. It is watching you and it is watching the rest of the hollow at the same time. It finds this sufficient.

### SENS-05 — The Deep Hearing

**Trigger:** Dark ↑ + Vibration-adjacent (Stress ↓, still environment)
**Method:** Sensory Overgrowth
**Severity:** subtle shift

**Event text:**
> It is hearing something you are not producing. The hollow is quiet, but it has oriented toward the floor with the focused attention of something tracking movement below. External ear structures have emerged — small, cartilaginous, uneven. It has not looked away from the floor in several minutes.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The ear structures settle into small, functional forms. The creature relaxes its attention from the floor. It still hears more than before, but within a manageable range. It does not appear distressed by what it hears.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The structures reduce. The creature lifts its head from the floor orientation. It is quiet for a long time. It returns to the floor occasionally over the next hours, as if checking whether what it heard is still there.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The structures develop further. The creature becomes very still — the deep stillness of something gathering information. Whatever it is hearing below, it is hearing more of it now. Its attention to the floor is permanent.

## CAVITY EXPANSION EVENTS
*Internal and oral body cavities enlarging into functional structures.*
*Primary trigger: Moist ↑ (L7/M7-T)*

---

### CAV-01 — The Palate Deepening

**Trigger:** Moist ↑ + Hunger ↑ (early adolescence)
**Method:** Cavity Expansion
**Severity:** subtle shift

**Event text:**
> The roof of its mouth has deepened. Not visibly from outside — you would not know unless it opened its mouth — but it does, briefly, and the interior space is larger than it was. It is secreting something mild and sweet-smelling that has not been present before. Small organisms near the floor of the hollow have begun moving toward it.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The palate stabilizes at its current depth. The secretion continues at a reduced, controlled rate. The creature closes its mouth and rests. The hollow smells faintly different. It will always smell like this now, in a quiet way.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The deepening stops. The secretion reduces quickly. The creature closes its mouth and does not open it again for some time. It watches the organisms on the floor move away from it with what might be indifference.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The palate continues to vault. The creature holds its mouth open longer, as if inviting. The secretion increases. The hollow smells distinctly sweet now. Something small has already entered and not left.

---

### CAV-02 — The Throat Chamber

**Trigger:** Moist ↑ + Pressure ↑ (adolescence)
**Method:** Cavity Expansion
**Severity:** marked change

**Event text:**
> There is a new hollow beneath its jaw. Not a growth — a deepening. A cavity has formed where the throat meets the chest, lined with tissue that is soft and receptive. The creature holds its head at an angle that presents this opening toward the warmth source. It is waiting for something to enter it.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The chamber settles into a defined, contained structure. The creature adjusts its posture and closes the opening partially. The cavity is functional but modest. It has learned what it has, and appears to accept the scale of it.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The chamber reduces. The creature pulls its head back to a neutral position. The cavity closes over. It sits very still for a long time, holding its jaw slightly open as if the memory of the opening is still present.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The chamber deepens. The creature raises its head to present the cavity more fully. The tissue lining it is receptive and warm. It holds this posture with complete patience. It has become an invitation.

### CAV-03 — The Rib Opening

**Trigger:** Moist ↑ + Temp ↑ + Pressure ↑ (adolescence, thermal swamp environment)
**Method:** Cavity Expansion
**Severity:** pronounced transformation

**Event text:**
> The ribcage has changed. The gaps between ribs have widened, and the tissue between them has become membranous rather than solid. The effect is not grotesque from the outside — the creature's outline has not dramatically changed — but when it breathes, the internal cavity is partially visible. It has been holding itself toward the hollow's moisture source for hours.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The ribcage settles with the membranous tissue intact but taut. The creature breathes deeply and evenly. The openings between ribs are present but bounded. From the right angle, in the right light, the cavity is visible. The creature seems entirely at ease with this.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The membranous tissue begins to thicken toward its prior solidity. The creature's posture shifts away from the moisture source. It presses its arms against its sides for a long time. It is not in distress. It is simply holding something closed.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The ribcage continues to open. The creature spreads its arms slightly, not to expose itself but to make room. The cavity is significant now. The hollow's moisture enters it freely. It breathes in a way that uses the whole interior.

### CAV-04 — The Stomach Vault

**Trigger:** Moist ↑ + Hunger ↑ (prolonged, adolescence)
**Method:** Cavity Expansion
**Severity:** marked change

**Event text:**
> Its abdomen has developed a new structure — a cavity just beneath the surface that opens when it presses against warm objects. It has been pressing against the hollow wall repeatedly with its midsection in a pattern that is not random. It is testing the opening's response to pressure and warmth.

**Stabilize reaction:**
> The vault settles into a contained and functional structure. The creature presses against the wall once more, slowly, and then steps back. The opening is modest. It will use it selectively, with intention.

**Suppress reaction:**
> The cavity reduces and the opening closes. The creature steps away from the wall and does not return to it with the same pressing motion. Something has been stopped. Whether it notices is unclear.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The vault deepens. The creature presses against the wall and holds. The opening responds to warmth by widening further. This is what it was building toward. It seems complete in a way it was not before.

### CAV-05 — The Nasal Filtration

**Trigger:** Moist ↑ + Clean ↓ (humid, neglected environment)
**Method:** Cavity Expansion
**Severity:** subtle shift

**Event text:**
> The nasal cavity has enlarged and the tissue inside it has become more complex — layered, receptive, capable of filtering more from the air it draws in. The creature has been breathing more slowly and deliberately than usual, as if testing the new intake. It is orienting toward areas of the hollow with higher moisture density.

**Stabilize reaction:**

**Let continue reaction:**

*Skin expanding, thickening, activating, or extending beyond the body.*
*Primary triggers: Temp ↑ + Clean ↓ (L7/M7-T)*

---

### DERM-01 — The Warmth Trap

**Trigger:** Temp ↑ + Hunger ↑ (early adolescence)
**Method:** Dermal Spread
**Severity:** subtle shift

**Event text:**
> Its skin has changed texture along the inner arms and chest — not visibly different in shape, but noticeably warmer to approach. The creature is pressing these surfaces toward the hollow's heat source with the attentiveness of something that has just discovered a tool. It retains heat longer than it did this morning.

**Stabilize reaction:**

**Suppress reaction:**
> The skin returns toward its prior thermal properties. The creature presses its arms against its sides and does not offer the surfaces toward the heat. It is cooler near it than it briefly was. Something has been made smaller.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The retention deepens. The creature's inner surfaces are now perceptibly warm at a distance. It extends its arms toward you with the patient quality of something that wants to share what it is generating. It is becoming a source, not just a receiver.

**Stabilize reaction:**

**Suppress reaction:**
> The extensions begin to retract. The creature pulls its limbs inward and holds itself compactly. The skin returns closer to its prior fit. It sits still for a long time, arms pressed to its sides.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The extensions continue. The creature spreads further. The skin that has separated from the underlying structure is significant now — it catches air when the creature moves, and the creature has begun moving in ways that use this. It is learning the new surface.

### DERM-04 — The Absorption Layer

**Method:** Dermal Spread

**Let continue reaction:**

### DERM-05 — The Coverage

**Trigger:** Temp ↑ + Stress ↑ + Clean ↓ (late adolescence, stress in hot environment)
**Method:** Dermal Spread
**Severity:** pronounced transformation

> The skin is extending over new structures — covering the joints, the face's margins, the spaces between limbs — not smoothing them but extending the dermal boundary outward. The creature looks slightly larger than it is. The effect is of something that has grown a new outer layer over its existing form. It holds very still and lets this happen.

**Stabilize reaction:**

**Suppress reaction:**
> The extension retracts. The creature shakes itself once — a full-body motion — and the excess skin draws back toward its prior distribution. It moves normally, but looks smaller than it briefly was. It sits near the warmth source and stays there.

**Let continue reaction:**
> The coverage continues. The creature's outer boundary has changed significantly. New surfaces have been claimed by the dermal layer. It stands fully upright and extends its limbs slowly, showing the full extent of what it has become. The hollow is not large enough for this to feel comfortable. It does not seem bothered.

## Usage Notes for M5 Implementation

**Event selection:** Events fire based on `biologicalPressure` threshold + dominant environment history (L7/Q9). Counter-based per-event sub-seed ensures `(event + consent choice)` is a fixed outcome (Q28/Q5).

**Presentation:** Show event text first. Then the three consent buttons with creature reaction text visible only after choice is made, or shown as preview — implementation decision for M5.

**One at a time:** While one event is pending, pressure accrual and stage advancement pause; care stats still drift (Q12/M5-L01).

**Strength/severity fiction (M5-L10):** Communicate severity through the event text's language ("small, hard formations" vs "the formations sharpen" vs "the ridge continues forward") — never show a number or percentage to the player.

**Fragment coupling:** After a Becoming resolves, the winning fragment(s) from this event should be selected from the Fragment Lexicon by matching Method + appropriate layer. The creature's label updates to reflect the committed trait.
