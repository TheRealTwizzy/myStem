# Visual Bible

## Core aesthetic

**Cozy with a wrong note.**

The world should feel intimate, tactile, warm-clinical, and biologically observant. The visual journey moves from genuine tenderness to eerie beauty without crossing into gore.

## Emotional target

- wonder;
- attachment;
- curiosity;
- care;
- faint unease;
- adult-scale awe;
- melancholy during strain;
- relief during recovery.

Not:

- disgust;
- splatter horror;
- cruelty;
- mascot comedy;
- heroic combat spectacle.

## Stemling visual law

Every Stemling must retain the fixed spawn plan.

Required:

- compact low quadruped;
- two eyes;
- four limbs;
- four digits per limb;
- three fingers and one opposable thumb per limb;
- no tail;
- no external ears;
- one mouth;
- two nasal openings;
- pale or softly warm tissue;
- size consistent with 32–42 cm nose to rump.

Lineage and premium status may not change spawn anatomy.

## Developed-form law

Every developed form must:

- derive from human anatomy;
- preserve structural history;
- avoid animal anatomy;
- show plausible support;
- use asymmetry intentionally;
- retain residual earlier-stage structures where plausible;
- read as one organism, not a collage of parts.

## Grotesqueness ceiling

Allowed:

- unusual eye multiplication;
- fused or elongated limbs;
- rib and sternal architecture;
- stretched membranes from human skin and fascia;
- visible but intact vascular patterns;
- internal-looking structures beneath translucent tissue;
- disproportion;
- resorbing residual structures;
- uncanny posture.

Forbidden:

- blood;
- open wounds;
- exposed raw flesh;
- traumatic severing;
- sexualized anatomy;
- animal cosplay;
- literal insect, fish, bird, reptile, or mammal parts.

## Symmetry

Perfect symmetry should be uncommon after Juvenile development.

Use asymmetry to express:

- environmental exposure;
- intervention history;
- structural compromise;
- support adaptation;
- lineage pressure;
- residual growth.

## Color

Color supports mood and biological clarity but never carries essential status alone.

Preferred world direction:

- warm pallor;
- amber;
- skin;
- bone;
- muted mineral;
- intimate shadow;
- low-saturation environmental accents.

## Free presentation — Hollow Field Sketch

- deterministic;
- generated on device;
- schematic but expressive;
- derived from authoritative anatomy;
- no permanent archive image;
- no implication of lower biological quality;
- transferable with the Field Entry.

## Premium presentation — Hollow Field Naturalism

- canonical premium style;
- one specimen;
- one approved pose family;
- one permanent image;
- no anatomical inset;
- no collage;
- no labels burned into the image;
- anatomy validated against the authoritative state;
- full-resolution archive asset;
- no reroll.

## Premium pose families

Launch target: 16 approved pose families.

A pose family must:

- reveal dominant anatomy;
- avoid hiding critical structures;
- fit stage and locomotion;
- avoid misleading symmetry;
- remain non-gory;
- support archive consistency.

## Scale communication

Stemling scale is intimate and compact.

Developed forms may vary greatly, but every illustration must include reliable scale logic through environment, pose, framing metadata, or archive description. Avoid accidental giant or miniature interpretation.

## Regression visuals

Regression should read as:

- settling;
- folding inward;
- resorption;
- soft detachment through biological withdrawal;
- reduced tension;
- simplified silhouette.

It must not resemble amputation.

## State visuals

Stable, Strained, Unstable, Decaying, and Recovery require:

- posture;
- movement;
- tissue behavior;
- icon;
- text label;
- optional color support.

## UI visual language

- portrait-first;
- large readable controls;
- minimal dense dashboards;
- clinical labels softened by tactile presentation;
- clear irreversible-action confirmations;
- no rarity frames;
- no loot-box visual grammar;
- no combat HUD.
