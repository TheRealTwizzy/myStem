# Biological Systems

## Fixed Stemling spawn anatomy

Every Stemling begins with the same unresolved anatomical plan.

### External

- 2 eyes
- 4 primary limbs
- 4 digits per limb
- 3 fingers plus 1 opposable thumb per limb
- no tail
- no external ears
- 1 mouth
- 2 nasal breathing openings
- no mature specialization

### Internal

- human-derived vertebral column
- 12 rib pairs
- 1 sternum
- 1 four-chambered heart
- 2 lungs
- 1 stomach
- human-pattern digestive tract
- 2 kidneys
- 1 brain
- 1 spinal cord

### Scale

- 32–42 cm nose to rump
- 2.5–4.5 kg
- low, compact quadrupedal posture
- approximately the size of a 6–12-month-old kitten

Seed type, lineage, and entitlement do not alter the visible spawn plan.

## Biological Signature

A Biological Signature is a hidden initialization object.

It may influence:

- region receptivity;
- mutation-operation weighting;
- tissue compatibility;
- support-component preference;
- asymmetry tendency;
- behavioral motifs;
- sustainment sensitivity;
- lineage influence response.

It must not encode a predetermined adult form.

## Anatomy graph

The authoritative body is represented as a graph of:

- anatomical regions;
- structures;
- tissue types;
- dependencies;
- support relationships;
- origin event;
- operation history;
- viability status;
- sustainment demands;
- residual visibility.

Every generated structure must trace to an allowed human-derived origin.

## Viability rules

A mutation result must satisfy:

- circulatory continuity;
- respiratory sufficiency;
- digestive continuity where relevant;
- neural control path;
- structural support;
- locomotion feasibility or stable immobility;
- tissue compatibility;
- intact skin or non-gory sheathing;
- no forbidden animal anatomy;
- no exact duplicate complete identity.

## Structural memory

Later growth may:

- exaggerate;
- duplicate;
- fuse;
- redirect;
- convert;
- integrate;
- reduce;
- support.

Earlier structures should remain visible when biologically plausible. Perfectly clean replacement is rare.

## Asymmetry

Perfect symmetry is uncommon after permanent differentiation.

Asymmetry may result from:

- uneven environmental exposure;
- prior intervention;
- lineage pressure;
- support limitations;
- region-specific Mutation Debt;
- local structural opportunity.

Asymmetry must remain viable and intentional, not appear as rendering error.

## Mutation Debt

Mutation Debt is hidden unresolved adaptive demand.

It may:

- bias a later operation;
- redirect development into a nearby region;
- alter support priority;
- reappear when matching environmental pressure returns.

Debt diminishes only when it meaningfully influences development and resolves only when resulting anatomy satisfies the unresolved drive.

## Sustainment

Each structure contributes demands to a hidden Sustainment Profile.

Demand domains include:

- thermal range;
- moisture range;
- light exposure;
- sub-biome-specific variable;
- oxygen and circulation;
- mechanical support;
- tissue hydration;
- mineral demand;
- movement opportunity;
- rest posture.

## Behavior

Launch target: 80 behavioral motifs.

Behavior must derive from anatomy and current state. It cannot be decorative randomness that contradicts biology.

## Sustainment archetypes

Launch target: 48 archetypes.

An archetype is an internal authoring pattern that groups compatible hidden demands. It must not become a visible class or species.

## Regression candidate selection

A removable mutation layer must:

- be topologically separable;
- leave a viable body;
- improve sustainment;
- preserve The Choosing;
- avoid traumatic visual interpretation;
- preserve permanent records without rewriting prior Field Entries.
