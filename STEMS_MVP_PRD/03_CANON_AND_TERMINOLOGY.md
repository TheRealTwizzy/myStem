# Canon and Terminology

## Canonical product name

**Stem**

## Organism terms

### Stemling

The fixed infant stage. Use only for the infant form.

### Stem

The general term for the organism at any stage. Also acceptable when stage is unknown or irrelevant.

### Juvenile

The first permanently differentiated stage.

### Adolescent

The volatile stage in which earlier adaptations intensify and The Choosing normally occurs.

### Settled Form

Adulthood. Settled does not mean mutation has ended; adult Becomings continue more slowly.

### Becoming

A major mutation event that reaches a player-facing consent decision and resolves into a new anatomical state.

### The Choosing

A unique Becoming, normally the third or fourth, occurring in late Juvenile or early Adolescent development. It commits the body to a dominant adaptive direction while preserving future variation.

### Mutation Consent

The three player responses to a Becoming:

- **Stabilize [+]**
- **Suppress [-]**
- **Let Continue [±]**

Do not rename these to cute, moralized, or overtly medical labels.

### Mutation Debt

Hidden unresolved adaptive demand created when a biological pressure is delayed, redirected, or incompletely satisfied. It is not a currency, punishment score, or visible stat.

### Regression

Permanent release of the least sustainable mutation layer after prolonged unrecovered Decay. Regression does not reverse lifecycle stage or The Choosing.

## Environment terms

### Hollow

A fixed care space containing one biome, one sub-biome, four environmental controls, fixed functional surfaces, and at most one living Stem.

Hollows are identified only by biome and slot number. Players do not assign custom Hollow names in the MVP.

### Biome family

One of seven high-level environment families.

### Sub-biome

One of three specialized environments within a family. Each sub-biome provides one unique five-band environmental variable.

## Record and economy terms

### Field Entry

A permanent record created immediately after a resolved Becoming. It contains biological metadata, presentation state, provenance, ownership, and one Genetic Entry.

### Genetic Entry

The single lineage-use unit embedded in a Field Entry. It may be unused or consumed. It is not detachable or independently tradable.

### Free Field Entry

A Field Entry using deterministic on-device **Hollow Field Sketch** presentation. It cannot be used for Lineage Synthesis.

### Premium Field Entry

A Field Entry with one permanent validated **Hollow Field Naturalism** illustration. It is archive-eligible and synthesis-eligible while its Genetic Entry remains unused.

### Canonical Illustration Allowance

A non-transferable subscription grant consumed to create one Premium illustration.

### Bio-Lab Analyst

The only MVP subscription tier.

### Illustrated Field Archive

A premium/grace view-only archive of validated Premium illustrations.

### Lineage Synthesis

Creation of a new Stemling from an eligible Premium Field Entry's Genetic Entry. It creates hidden lineage influence, not a clone.

### New Seed

Free creation of a genetically unrelated Stemling in an empty Hollow.

## Biological identity terms

### Biological Signature

The hidden initialization package defining an individual Stem's latent biological tendencies. It never changes the fixed infant spawn plan and is never shown directly.

### Mutation component

An authored anatomical operation unit applied to a region under biological constraints.

### Support or compensatory component

An authored structure added to sustain, integrate, stabilize, or make another mutation viable.

### Sustainment Profile

The hidden adult care demands produced by accumulated anatomy.

## State terms

Canonical biological conditions:

1. Stable
2. Strained
3. Unstable
4. Decaying
5. Regressed / immediate recovery

“Dead,” “dying,” “injured,” and “amputated” are not ordinary-care states.

## Lore language

Preferred:

- specimen;
- observation;
- sustainment;
- structure;
- tissue;
- pressure;
- response;
- signature;
- consent;
- field record;
- settling;
- becoming.

Avoid:

- monster type;
- breed;
- species unlock;
- evolution tree;
- rarity;
- hatch;
- pet storage;
- inventory creature;
- breeding pair;
- genetic recipe.

## Legacy terminology

The retired term **Neepet** must not appear in current product-facing documents.

Earlier mutation labels such as Compression, Ossification, Sensory Overgrowth, Membrane Extension, Cavity Expansion, Connective-Tissue Dominance, and Dermal Spread may be retained only as **expression profiles, visual biases, or legacy vocabulary**.

The canonical component-operation taxonomy is:

- Proliferation
- Reduction
- Duplication
- Fusion
- Reorientation
- Conversion
- Integration
