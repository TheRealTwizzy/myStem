# Mutation Methods and Components

## Canonical operation taxonomy

Every authored mutation component uses one primary operation:

1. **Proliferation** — growth or multiplication of existing tissue.
2. **Reduction** — controlled decrease, resorption, or simplification.
3. **Duplication** — copying of a structure or functional unit.
4. **Fusion** — joining structures into a new integrated unit.
5. **Reorientation** — changing direction, alignment, articulation, or load path.
6. **Conversion** — transforming tissue into another human-derived biological role.
7. **Integration** — adding support, dependency, or coordinated function between structures.

These operations describe what happens anatomically.

## Legacy expression profiles

The following terms may be used as visual or environmental expression profiles, not as the component-operation taxonomy:

- Compression
- Ossification
- Sensory Overgrowth
- Membrane Extension
- Cavity Expansion
- Connective-Tissue Dominance
- Dermal Spread

A component may have one operation and multiple expression tags.

## Component schema

Every component must define:

- component ID;
- display-safe internal name;
- anatomical region;
- human-derived origin;
- primary operation;
- compatible stages;
- prerequisite structures;
- incompatible structures;
- environmental drivers;
- lineage influence hooks;
- Mutation Debt hooks;
- resulting anatomy;
- support needs;
- sustainment demands;
- residual behavior;
- visual descriptors;
- biofeedback cues;
- regression removability;
- AI illustration constraints;
- test fixtures.

## Component classes

### Base

Primary visible anatomical change.

### Transitional

Bridges one body state to another, often during Juvenile or Adolescent development.

### Hero

High-salience component with strong silhouette, behavior, or illustration impact.

### Support / compensatory

Makes another component viable, stable, controlled, or sustainable.

Player-facing systems must never expose these production classes as rarity.

## Selection pipeline

1. Determine developmental stage and Becoming context.
2. Evaluate current Hollow pressure and historical exposure.
3. Load current anatomy graph.
4. Apply hidden Biological Signature and lineage influence.
5. Apply unresolved Mutation Debt.
6. identify eligible anatomical regions.
7. select one or more compatible operations.
8. add required support components.
9. validate viability.
10. score novelty and coherence.
11. produce biofeedback and observation text.
12. commit anatomy atomically.

## Becoming composition

A Becoming should normally include:

- one dominant change;
- zero to several support changes;
- visible connection to current or historical pressure;
- at least one consequence or sustainment implication;
- retained evidence of earlier anatomy.

## Stabilize effect

Stabilize should:

- narrow eligible variation;
- favor coherent Integration and necessary support;
- reduce unresolved debt creation;
- increase future sustainment specificity;
- never guarantee a preferred appearance.

## Suppress effect

Suppress may:

- reduce visible completion;
- redirect operation;
- increase Reduction;
- defer pressure;
- create or intensify Mutation Debt;
- require later compensation.

## Let Continue effect

Let Continue should preserve the broadest valid operation set.

It is not neglect and should not receive a hidden penalty.

## The Choosing

The Choosing selects and commits a dominant adaptive direction based on accumulated history. It should strongly weight future compatibility without freezing exact anatomy.

## Novelty rules

The system should prevent exact complete-identity duplication through:

- component combination diversity;
- parameter variation;
- asymmetry;
- support-path differences;
- history;
- behavior;
- residual structures;
- visual manifestation.

Convergent function is allowed. Identical complete organisms are not.

## Forbidden authoring patterns

- direct animal-part substitution;
- “upgrade” ladders;
- rarity labels;
- clean species templates;
- deterministic recipe mapping;
- premium-only mutation components;
- components that require gore to read;
- support components with no causal role;
- mutations that ignore prior anatomy.

## Authoring acceptance checklist

A component is ready only when:

- its origin is human-derived;
- viability is documented;
- it has at least two environmental contexts;
- it includes support implications;
- it has multimodal feedback;
- it is illustration-safe;
- it can participate in at least one fixture;
- it does not imply a fixed species.
