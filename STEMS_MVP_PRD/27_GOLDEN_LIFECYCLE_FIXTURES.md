# Golden Lifecycle Fixtures

## Purpose

Golden fixtures provide deterministic, reviewable lifecycle histories used to verify canon, simulation, content, UI, analytics, and premium illustration constraints.

Each fixture must define:

- seed;
- Biological Signature;
- Hollow and history;
- Consent choices;
- expected operation classes;
- support structures;
- stage transitions;
- The Choosing;
- sustainment;
- Field Entries;
- expected biofeedback;
- regression behavior where applicable;
- illustration constraints.

## Fixture 1 — Temperate Stabilized Growth

Hollow:

- Temperate Grove / Canopy Basin

Path:

- early Airflow increase;
- Becoming 1 Stabilize;
- Becoming 2 Let Continue;
- Choosing at Becoming 3 Stabilize;
- Settled at Becoming 5.

Assertions:

- coherent Integration;
- minimal Debt;
- one Field Entry per Becoming;
- adult remains asymmetrical;
- no animal wing anatomy.

## Fixture 8 — Adult Regression

Hollow:

- Industrial Ruin / Radiant Foundry

Assertions:

- least sustainable removable layer selected;
- The Choosing preserved;
- stage preserved;
- no Field Entry created;
- remaining Sustainment Profile easier;
- visual language is resorption, not amputation;
- post-regression release temporarily blocked.

## Fixture 9 — Premium Lineage Economy

Source:

- Premium Field Entry from a settled adult;
- Genetic Entry unused.

Path:

- paid or grace synthesis;
- create new Stemling in Peat Bog;
- transfer source Field Entry after consumption.

Assertions:

- Genetic Entry consumes once;
- new Stemling uses fixed anatomy;
- hidden lineage influence exists;
- no visible inherited trait at spawn;
- source image remains attached;
- consumed state follows ownership;
- failed retry does not create second Stemling.

## Cross-fixture invariants

All nine must verify:

- exactly three Hollows maximum;
- no offline permanent action;
- no direct anatomy selection;
- no fixed species;
- no premium pacing advantage;
- no gore;
- all permanent transactions idempotent;
- all Field Entries have correct provenance;
- all critical signals have accessible equivalents.
