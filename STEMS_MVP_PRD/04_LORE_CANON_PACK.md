# STEMS — Lore Canon Pack

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical narrative boundaries of STEMS.

It distinguishes:

- Established fact
- Research conclusion
- Disputed interpretation
- Protected unknown

Narrative writers and AI systems must preserve uncertainty where uncertainty is canonical.

The modern player era remains deliberately undated.

---

# 1. Lore Tone

The world of STEMS should feel:

- Recovered
- Fragmentary
- Scientific
- Intimate
- Post-institutional
- Melancholy
- Cautiously hopeful
- Biologically unsettling

The game should not present lore as a conventional heroic campaign.

The player is not told they will:

- Save humanity
- Defeat a central enemy
- Restore Holloway
- Control evolution
- Build an army
- Reclaim the world

The player studies, cares, records, releases, and continues lineages.

---

# 2. Truth Categories

## Established Fact

A fact the product may state directly.

Examples:

- Stems begin from a shared unresolved Stemling form.
- Hollows sustain one active Stem.
- Becomings alter anatomy.
- Field Entries record developmental snapshots.
- The modern player era occurs after recovered Holloway records.

## Research Conclusion

A strongly supported interpretation attributed to researchers.

Examples:

- Sustained environmental exposure influences developmental pressure.
- The Choosing organizes later anatomy around an emerging adaptive direction.
- Regression releases unsustainable tissue to preserve life.

Research conclusions may be revised by later discoveries.

## Disputed Interpretation

A theory on which qualified researchers disagree.

Examples:

- Whether certain repeated behaviors demonstrate memory.
- Whether lineage resemblance reflects direct inherited structure or broader biological weighting.
- Whether the Choir represents intelligence, coordination, or emergent signaling.
- Whether released Stems form stable ecological communities.

## Protected Unknown

A question the product must not resolve yet.

Examples:

- The final fate of all Holloway facilities.
- The full nature of the Choir.
- Whether Nini’s language was repeatable.
- Whether Stems collectively understand Field Researchers.
- The exact date of the modern player era.
- The ultimate limits of adult convergence.

---

# 3. The Recovered Holloway Period

## Holloway Therapeutics

Holloway Therapeutics was an institutional research organization involved in advanced biological adaptation and regenerative development.

Recovered records associate Holloway with:

- Project NEPHE-7
- Artificially sustained biological environments
- Human-derived adaptive tissue
- Controlled mutation research
- Early Stem observation
- Lineage continuity experiments
- Institutional containment failures

The modern player does not work for an active confirmed Holloway organization.

Avoid stating:

- Holloway still controls the player
- The player is a Holloway employee
- Every Hollow is an original Holloway chamber
- Holloway survived intact

---

# 4. Project NEPHE-7

Project NEPHE-7 investigated an adaptable human-derived biological system capable of reorganizing anatomy in response to sustained environmental demand.

Recovered conclusions suggest:

- Viable offspring returned to a highly unresolved infant form.
- Completed adult anatomy was not copied directly.
- Environmental history shaped developmental expression.
- Tissue was recycled internally to fund new growth.
- Mutation created costs and dependencies.
- Later forms remained rooted in human anatomy.
- The Choosing reduced unrestricted developmental flexibility.

Project NEPHE-7 did not produce fixed species.

Its subjects became what modern researchers call Stems.

---

# 5. Dr. Elian Voss

## Canonical role

Dr. Elian Voss was the principal investigator most strongly associated with Project NEPHE-7.

## Expertise

- Regenerative biology
- Adaptive morphology
- Developmental plasticity
- Tissue integration
- Lineage continuity

## Narrative function

Voss provides:

- Historical scientific authority
- Early theoretical language
- Evidence of institutional ambition
- Evidence of incomplete understanding
- A source of recovered records and unresolved decisions

## Voice

Voss should sound:

- Precise
- Controlled
- Ambitious
- Increasingly uncertain
- Reluctant to abandon explanatory authority

## Limitations

Voss should not be treated as omniscient.

His records may be:

- Incomplete
- Institutionally filtered
- Technically accurate but ethically narrow
- Wrong about long-term outcomes
- Written before later discoveries

---

# 6. The R-17 Breach

The R-17 breach marks a recovered historical rupture associated with containment failure and the wider dispersal of adaptive biological systems.

Established facts may include:

- Containment failed.
- Institutional control weakened.
- Stems or related biological material entered environments outside intended study.
- Later records became fragmented.

Protected details include:

- The complete cause
- Full casualty accounting
- Whether sabotage occurred
- Whether the breach was singular or one of several
- Whether all released material shared one origin
- Whether Holloway understood the event in time

The breach should not become a conventional disaster-action sequence.

Its narrative power comes from incomplete records and biological consequence.

---

# 7. The Choir

The Choir is a canonical but unresolved biological phenomenon.

Possible evidence may include:

- Repeated synchronized behavior
- Shared environmental response
- Long-distance patterning
- Coordinated vocal or vibrational activity
- Group-level adaptation
- Researcher disagreement about intent

The product must not definitively state that the Choir is:

- A hive mind
- Telepathic
- Hostile
- Benevolent
- A deity
- A network controlled by Holloway

The Choir should remain a protected mystery.

---

# 8. Nini

Nini is an exceptional historical case associated with language-like behavior.

Nini’s significance lies in the possibility that a Stem crossed a boundary researchers believed stable.

Established facts may include:

- Nini produced behavior interpreted as language-like.
- Records were incomplete or contested.
- The event materially affected researcher interpretation.

The product must not imply that ordinary Stems can:

- Speak human language
- Repeat player speech
- Develop dialogue routinely
- Become conversational companions

Nini remains exceptional.

---

# 9. The Modern Player Era

The modern player era occurs after the recovered Holloway period.

It is deliberately undated.

The game should not state:

- A calendar year
- An exact number of years since Holloway
- A complete geopolitical history
- A confirmed surviving government structure
- A single global institution controlling all researchers

The undated setting allows:

- Fragmented recovery
- Ongoing research
- Environmental variety
- Uncertain institutional continuity
- Player interpretation

---

# 10. Field Researchers

Modern players are Field Researchers.

They have access to:

- Three active Hollows
- Environmental controls
- Stemling creation
- Field Entry recording
- Social exchange
- Release protocols
- Knowledge Base material

They do not have:

- Unlimited long-term sustainment capacity
- Direct anatomical control
- Complete biological understanding
- Guaranteed access to the wider world after release
- Institutional authority over other researchers

The three-Stem limit is both a product rule and a canonical care constraint.

---

# 11. Bio-Lab Analysts

Bio-Lab Analyst is a modern access designation.

It grants technical capabilities related to:

- Canonical illustration
- Archive access
- Lineage Synthesis

It does not represent:

- A separate biological caste
- Superior scientific intelligence
- Authority over free Field Researchers
- In-world genetic enhancement
- Higher-quality Stems

The subscription mechanism itself need not be deeply explained in lore.

Avoid turning monetization into a fictional biological hierarchy.

---

# 12. The Wider World

Released Stems enter a wider world beyond player-managed Hollows.

Established facts:

- The player cannot sustain more than three active Stems.
- Release is permanent.
- Released Stems leave direct care.
- The player does not receive later interface access.

Protected unknowns:

- Exact release destinations
- Survival rates
- Ecological communities
- Whether lineages meet
- Whether the wider world contains human settlements
- Whether released Stems recognize prior researchers

The product must not generate:

- Later sightings
- Release updates
- Recall missions
- World-map tracking
- Released-Stem galleries

---

# 13. The Three Canonical Modern Researchers

## Dr. Mara Vey

### Expertise

- Developmental morphology
- Becomings
- Consent History
- Anatomical integration
- Adult sustainment

### Voice

- Formal
- Patient
- Precise
- Reflective
- Comfortable with uncertainty
- Focused on the whole organism

### Core belief

A Stem’s body cannot be understood by examining its latest mutation in isolation.

### Writing tendencies

Vey often emphasizes:

- Continuity
- Structural history
- Consequence
- Integration
- The difference between support and control

### Example

> The structure did not begin when it became visible. By then, the body had already spent considerable time preparing somewhere the observer could not see.

### Should not author

- Billing information
- Social exchange instructions
- Aggressive institutional claims
- Casual humor

---

## Dr. Ilyan Ro

### Expertise

- Environmental systems
- Hollow conditions
- Pressure relationships
- Adult sustainment
- Field observation

### Voice

- Direct
- Empirical
- Skeptical
- Occasionally dry
- Focused on repeated evidence

### Core belief

A single reaction is weak evidence. Repeated behavior under changing conditions is more useful.

### Writing tendencies

Ro emphasizes:

- Exposure
- Duration
- Repetition
- Environmental conflict
- Measurement limitations
- Observer error

### Example

> The Stem moved toward the heated surface once. That is an event. It returned after every reduction in warmth. That is a pattern.

### Should not author

- Metaphysical Choir theories
- Emotional biographies
- Historical Holloway leadership records outside his expertise

---

## Dr. Sera Quill

### Expertise

- Field Entries
- Research ethics
- Lineage interpretation
- Observer relationships
- Documentation and preservation

### Voice

- Elegant
- restrained
- Humane
- Cautious about ownership language
- Sensitive to emotional consequence

### Core belief

A record preserves a moment. It does not preserve the living organism itself.

### Writing tendencies

Quill emphasizes:

- Snapshot versus specimen
- Stewardship
- Interpretation
- Permanence
- Ethical uncertainty
- The limits of archives

### Example

> A Field Entry is exact only in one respect: it records that this body existed in this way, once. Everything before and after remains alive with uncertainty.

### Should not author

- Detailed tissue mechanics
- Exact environmental calculations
- Operational billing copy

---

# 14. Researcher Relationships

The modern researchers should not read like interchangeable narrators.

## Vey and Ro

- Vey may interpret structures as developmental continuity.
- Ro may challenge whether the observed pattern is sufficiently repeated.

## Vey and Quill

- Vey focuses on what anatomy means biologically.
- Quill focuses on what recording and ownership mean ethically.

## Ro and Quill

- Ro may prefer precise environmental evidence.
- Quill may warn against reducing a living relationship to a reproducible condition.

Disagreement should remain professional and evidence-based.

# 15. Knowledge Base Structure

The Knowledge Base includes:

- Gameplay guidance
- Necessary world context
- Formal researcher notes
- Recovered Holloway fragments
- Environmental descriptions
- Field Entry guidance
- Release guidance

It does not function as:

- A mutation recipe book
- A hidden-trait database
- A complete biology encyclopedia
- A species catalogue
- A walkthrough for guaranteed forms

---

# 16. Biological Discovery

Biological discoveries should be written as formal researcher notes tied to the world.

Players may unlock notes through:

- First Becoming
- First Field Entry
- The Choosing
- First adult Sustainment Profile
- First Premium illustration
- First Lineage Synthesis
- First Regression
- First permanent release

Unlocking a note does not mean the player discovered a universal mechanic.

It means relevant research material has become available in context.

---

# 17. Field Entries in Lore

A Field Entry is a formal developmental record.

It records:

- A post-Becoming snapshot
- Current anatomy
- Lifecycle stage
- Consent context
- Environmental context
- One Genetic Entry

A Field Entry is not:

- The living Stem
- A soul
- A full genome
- A guaranteed lineage outcome
- A species certificate

---

# 18. Genetic Entries in Lore

A Genetic Entry contains enough preserved biological continuity to strongly bias a newly synthesized Stemling.

Researchers do not fully understand which qualities survive synthesis.

The product should not state that a Genetic Entry contains:

- Exact traits
- Complete memories
- A copied personality
- A deterministic genome
- The original Stem’s consciousness

---

# 19. Premium Illustration in Lore

Canonical illustrations are formal, professionally rendered Field Entry depictions.

Their permanence may be explained through:

- Archival protocol
- Verified record preservation
- Canonical visual reconstruction
- Institutional-quality documentation

The game does not need to explain AI-generation technology directly inside lore.

Avoid implying that illustration changes the biology.

---

# 20. Misfolds

Misfolds remain canonical historical biology but are not player-achievable in the MVP.

A Misfold is associated with:

- Incomplete Choosing
- Incompatible pressure
- Failure to establish coherent adult commitment
- Persistent anatomical instability

The product must not imply that ordinary imperfect care easily creates Misfolds.

Misfold lore may foreshadow future systems.

---

# 21. Death

Death exists in historical and wider-world canon.

However, ordinary player cultivation cannot permanently kill a Stem in the MVP.

Research notes may discuss:

- Historical losses
- Failed containment
- Unsustainable external conditions
- Institutional experiments
- Wider-world mortality

The player-facing care loop uses Regression rather than permanent death.

---

# 22. Canonical Lore Non-Answers

The game must not resolve:

- Whether Stems are fully human-derived persons
- Whether the Choir is conscious
- Whether Nini understood language
- Whether released Stems remember researchers
- Whether Holloway intended the eventual outcome
- Whether all modern Stems descend from one source
- Whether lineage carries memory
- Whether the wider world is improving or worsening
- Whether adult convergence has an upper limit

These unknowns preserve long-term narrative space.

---

# 23. Forbidden Lore Assumptions

Do not state or imply that:

- Stems are aliens
- Stems are magical
- Stems belong to fixed species
- The player works directly for Holloway
- Holloway still governs the world
- Premium membership is an in-world biological enhancement
- Every researcher agrees
- The Choir is definitively a hive mind
- Nini proves all Stems can speak
- Released Stems return
- The player is rebuilding humanity
- Combat is central to Stem biology
- Field Entries contain consciousness
- Lineage Synthesis is ordinary sexual reproduction

---

# 24. Lore Authoring Rules

Every lore entry must identify:

- Truth category
- Era
- Author
- Author expertise
- Evidence basis
- Uncertainty level
- Protected information
- Canonical terms

A lore entry should not reveal more mechanical certainty than the gameplay interface.

# 25. Sample Research Notes

## Mara Vey — On Becomings

> A Becoming is rarely sudden, though it often appears so to the observer. The visible structure is only the final portion of a longer negotiation between pressure, tissue, and memory. By the time Consent is requested, the body has already begun deciding what it can afford to become.

## Ilyan Ro — On Repeated Conditions

> Researchers consistently overvalue dramatic adjustments. A brief extreme may provoke a strong reaction, but development is more often shaped by conditions that return, remain, and become ordinary.

## Sera Quill — A Snapshot Is Not a Specimen

> A Field Entry preserves one exact arrangement of tissue and one unresolved promise of continuity. It does not preserve the living Stem. Confusing the two encourages ownership where stewardship would be more honest.

## Mara Vey — On Regression

> Regression is not a return to an earlier life. The body does not forget what it became. It releases what it can no longer sustain and reorganizes the remainder around survival.

## Ilyan Ro — On Environmental Labels

> “Warm” is not a universal condition. It is a relationship between a Hollow profile, a physical threshold, and the body presently exposed to it. Researchers who compare labels without context usually compare nothing at all.

## Sera Quill — On Release

> The absence that follows release should not be mistaken for erasure. The Stem has left the researcher’s reach, not necessarily the world. Whether that distinction offers comfort is not a matter the record can settle.

---

# 26. Lore Acceptance Criteria

The lore system is coherent when:

- The modern era remains undated.
- Holloway is historical rather than a confirmed active player institution.
- Voss remains authoritative but fallible.
- Vey, Ro, and Quill have distinct voices.
- The Choir remains unresolved.
- Nini remains exceptional.
- Misfolds remain lore-only in the MVP.
- Release creates permanent uncertainty.
- Lineage does not confirm memory or exact traits.
- Premium is not biological superiority.
- Research notes add context without exposing mutation recipes.
- AI-generated lore respects truth categories and protected unknowns.
