# Biofeedback Language System

## Purpose

Biofeedback lets the player understand broad biological need and cause without exposing hidden values, recipes, or predicted mutations.

## Required channels

Every critical condition must use at least two channels, preferably three or more:

1. Posture
2. Movement
3. Breathing or internal rhythm
4. Tissue behavior
5. Environmental interaction
6. Non-verbal sound
7. Concise observational text

## Design rules

Biofeedback must be:

- causally grounded;
- multimodal;
- color-independent;
- readable without sound;
- readable with reduced motion;
- concise;
- non-numeric;
- non-prescriptive about exact outcomes.

## Observation voice

Tone:

- warm-clinical;
- specific;
- non-judgmental;
- slightly uncanny;
- biologically plausible.

Preferred structure:

> observed change → probable broad need → uncertainty retained

Example:

> “The rib margin is holding closer to the warm surface. Its breathing settles when the Hollow rises above cool.”

Avoid:

> “Set Warmth to 4 to unlock rib expansion.”

## Hidden values

Never reveal:

- exact thresholds;
- formulas;
- percentages;
- mutation probabilities;
- Mutation Debt quantities;
- lineage weights;
- optimal Hollow settings;
- guaranteed component outcomes.

## Signal strength

### Subtle

Used for early drift and low-risk preference.

### Clear

Used for meaningful mismatch or support need.

### Urgent

Used for Instability, Decay, intervention failure risk, or imminent regression.

Urgency must not rely on red color or alarm sound alone.

## Channel examples

### Posture

- curling toward heat;
- flattening under pressure;
- avoiding bright surfaces;
- bracing a new structure;
- protecting an overburdened region.

### Movement

- shortened gait;
- repeated repositioning;
- delayed extension;
- asymmetrical reach;
- stillness followed by correction.

### Rhythm

- breathing cadence;
- visible vascular pulse;
- swallow interval;
- tissue contraction;
- rest-cycle interruption.

### Tissue behavior

- tightening;
- swelling;
- soft folding;
- mineral settling;
- membrane tension;
- vascular brightness;
- controlled resorption.

### Environmental interaction

- seeking a particular surface;
- avoiding current;
- pressing into substrate;
- orienting toward dimness;
- holding beneath drip or airflow.

### Sound

- soft coo;
- wet click;
- breathy pulse;
- low hum;
- tension creak;
- rhythmic throat note.

All sounds require captions or text equivalents.

## Consent communication

Before a Becoming choice, the system must explain:

- what biological process appears to be occurring;
- what Stabilize asks the player to maintain;
- what Suppress asks the player to oppose;
- that Let Continue permits natural resolution;
- that exact outcome is uncertain.

Do not frame one option as morally correct.

## Adult sustainment warnings

Warnings should identify:

- affected anatomical area;
- broad environmental mismatch;
- severity;
- whether recovery remains available.

Do not state exact time-to-regression.

## Regression communication

Regression must be preceded by escalating signals and a direct warning while online.

The final event should be described as structural release, resorption, or settling—not injury or punishment.

## Accessibility

Every critical animation requires a text equivalent. Every sound requires a caption. Every status color requires icon, shape, and label support.

## Content inventory target

Launch target: 400–500 observation templates, parameterized by:

- stage;
- anatomy region;
- condition;
- environmental variable;
- severity;
- intervention state;
- recovery state.
