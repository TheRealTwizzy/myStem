# STEMS — Core Gameplay Loop

## Status

**Document type:** Canonical gameplay specification  
**Release scope:** MVP  
**Primary audience:** Product, game design, UX, engineering, analytics, QA, narrative, and AI-assisted production

---

# Purpose

This document defines what players repeatedly do in STEMS.

The game centers on:

- Creating unresolved Stemlings
- Observing biological behavior
- Adjusting environmental conditions
- Interpreting cause and effect
- Responding during Becomings
- Preserving developmental snapshots
- Managing three active lives
- Releasing Stems permanently
- Beginning new generations

The game is not organized around combat, experience points, collectible species, or direct anatomy selection.

---

# Core Player Fantasy

> I sustain a living organism whose development I can influence, study, preserve, and sometimes continue—but never fully control.

The player should feel:

- Responsible for conditions
- Curious about behavior
- Attached to individual Stems
- Unsure of exact outcomes
- Capable of learning
- Aware that decisions have lasting consequences

---

# Primary Loop

```text
Choose an empty Hollow
        ↓
Choose a biome family
        ↓
Choose one of its three sub-biomes
        ↓
Choose New Seed or Lineage Synthesis
        ↓
Observe the unresolved Stemling
        ↓
Adjust Hollow conditions
        ↓
Interpret immediate and repeated responses
        ↓
Accumulate environmental and biological history
        ↓
Encounter a Becoming
        ↓
Choose Stabilize, Suppress, or Let Continue
        ↓
Support the resulting biological process
        ↓
Receive a Field Entry
        ↓
Continue cultivation
        ↓
Reach adult sustainment and later Becomings
        ↓
Release a Stem when care capacity must change
        ↓
Begin another life
```

# Player Time Horizons

STEMS supports several session lengths without making one mandatory.

## Brief observation visit

Approximate intent:

- Inspect one or more Stems
- Read a current observation
- Confirm whether any meaningful response is waiting
- Leave without changing conditions

A brief visit should not be punished for its brevity.

## Intentional care session

Approximate intent:

- Observe behavior
- Review current Hollow conditions
- Make one or more connected adjustments
- Wait for or inspect immediate response
- Compare behavior with recent patterns

## Major biological session

Approximate intent:

- Resolve a Becoming
- Perform Stabilization or Suppression
- Review a new Field Entry
- Complete Lineage Synthesis
- Release a Stem
- Complete a trade or gift

The game must not require long uninterrupted sessions for routine survival.

---

# Meaningful Session

A meaningful session includes at least one authoritative interaction or observation that contributes to the player’s understanding or the Stem’s history.

Qualifying examples:

- Observing a newly available biological signal
- Adjusting a Hollow and seeing a committed response
- Comparing repeated behavior across sessions
- Resolving a Becoming
- Maintaining Stabilization
- Maintaining Suppression
- Reviewing a new Field Entry
- Creating a Stemling
- Completing release
- Recovering a Strained, Unstable, or Decaying Stem

Non-qualifying examples:

- Opening and immediately closing the application
- Viewing a static menu
- Repeatedly moving controls before server commitment
- Refreshing a trade listing
- Browsing the Illustrated Field Archive without cultivating a Stem

Analytics may distinguish meaningful cultivation from general product engagement.

# First-Time Player Loop

## 1. Establish Field Researcher identity

The player receives:

- A display name
- A public account identifier
- Three permanently numbered Hollow slots
- Required first-time tutorial guidance

The player is introduced as a **Field Researcher**.

---

## 2. Select Hollow 1

The first active slot is:

> Hollow 1 — Empty

Hollows 2 and 3 become available after the first Becoming.

This is onboarding progression, not a paid unlock.

---

## 3. Select a biome family

The player sees seven canonical biome families:

1. Temperate Grove
2. Wetland
3. Littoral Coast
4. Arid Expanse
5. Alpine Shelf
6. Subterranean Cavern
7. Industrial Ruin

Family selection presents environmental identity without showing likely creature outcomes.

---

## 4. Select a sub-biome

The player sees the selected family’s three canonical sub-biomes.

The screen communicates:

- Unique environmental variable
- Starting conditions
- Physical surface character
- Biome permanence during the active Stem’s life

It does not communicate:

- Mutation probabilities
- Difficulty
- Recommended anatomy
- Species outcomes
- Lineage compatibility

---

## 5. Create a New Seed Stemling

The first creation path is New Seed.

Lineage Synthesis is not relevant because the player owns no Field Entry.

The server:

1. Reserves Hollow 1.
2. Assigns the selected biome profile.
3. Initializes starting conditions.
4. Creates a random Biological Signature.
5. Creates the canonical unresolved body.
6. Saves the Stemling.
7. Commits the occupied Hollow.

---

## 6. Observe before adjusting

The player initially observes:

- Resting posture
- Breathing
- Eye tracking
- Surface contact
- Initial movement
- Environmental orientation

The first tutorial instruction should be:

> Observe before changing the Hollow.

The goal is to teach that behavior carries information.

---

## 7. Make the first Hollow adjustment

The tutorial introduces one universal control first:

- Warmth
- Moisture
- Or Light

The selected sub-biome’s unique variable is introduced next.

The player sees:

- Pending adjustment
- Server confirmation
- Gradual environmental transition
- Stem response

No response is presented as a guaranteed mutation outcome.

---

## 8. Return across meaningful sessions

The first Becoming occurs:

- During the first active day, or
- After approximately two to three meaningful sessions

The player sees escalating foreshadowing before Consent is requested.

---

## 9. Resolve the first Becoming

The player chooses:

- Stabilize
- Suppress
- Let Continue

The result:

- Changes the body
- Creates future consequences
- Transitions the Stemling into Juvenile development
- Produces the first Field Entry
- Unlocks Hollows 2 and 3

---

# Ongoing Cultivation Loop

After onboarding, each active Stem repeatedly moves through:

```text
Observe
↓
Form a theory
↓
Adjust or maintain conditions
↓
Observe response
↓
Compare with past behavior
↓
Encounter new pressure
↓
Resolve a Becoming
↓
Adapt care to the resulting body
```

The loop should never become:

```text
Read exact requirement
↓
Set exact values
↓
Wait for guaranteed result
```

---

# Observation Loop

Observation is the central player verb.

The player watches:

- Posture
- Movement
- Breathing
- Tissue behavior
- Surface selection
- Environmental avoidance
- Repetition
- Appetite
- Recovery
- Interaction with the observation boundary

The system supports interpretation through concise observational text.

Text must describe evidence rather than reveal hidden calculations.

---

# Hollow Adjustment Loop

Every occupied Hollow exposes:

- Warmth
- Moisture
- Light
- One sub-biome-specific variable

A player may:

- Increase or decrease a target
- Maintain existing conditions
- Compare responses across surfaces
- Create stable exposure
- Create environmental contrast
- Create controlled variation

A player may not:

- Queue offline adjustments
- View exact numeric values
- See mutation probabilities
- Select a target anatomy
- Purchase stronger environmental controls

---

# Immediate Response Loop

After a committed adjustment, the Stem may show:

- Relaxation
- Withdrawal
- Seeking
- Guarding
- Changed breathing
- Changed tissue tension
- Increased or reduced activity
- Surface migration

Immediate response means:

> The Stem noticed and responded to the change.

It does not mean:

> The player selected a developmental path.

---

# Repeated Pattern Loop

A behavior becomes more meaningful when it:

- Repeats
- Persists across sessions
- Returns after environmental changes
- Localizes around one anatomical region
- Appears under similar pressure
- Resembles lineage history

The player should gradually become better at identifying patterns without receiving exact mechanical confirmation.

---

# Becoming Loop

A Becoming begins after sufficient biological pressure and developmental readiness.

The product:

- Foreshadows the event
- Waits safely for player Consent
- Presents the three choices together
- Explains action rather than outcome
- Prevents overlapping high-attention biological events

Only one active high-attention Becoming intervention may run account-wide.

---

# Stabilization Loop

After selecting Stabilize:

1. The player inspects emerging anatomy.
2. The player adjusts or maintains supportive conditions.
3. The structure responds.
4. Support may strengthen.
5. Commitment becomes qualitatively visible.
6. Direct intervention eventually ends.
7. Natural development completes the Becoming.

Stabilization includes one Resume Support opportunity before commitment.

Network interruption:

- Pauses safely
- Does not consume Resume Support
- Cannot be exploited indefinitely

---

# Suppression Loop

After selecting Suppress:

1. The player identifies conditions that oppose the emerging change.
2. The player maintains opposing exposure.
3. The structure may retract or resist.
4. Pressure may redirect.
5. Suppression either succeeds, partially succeeds, or fails.
6. Active Suppression cannot restart during the same Becoming.

Suppression may create Mutation Debt.

It does not guarantee a clean reset.

---

# Let Continue Loop

After selecting Let Continue:

- The player may continue ordinary care
- The Stem remains influenced by current conditions
- No direct support or resistance state is created
- Broader natural variability remains available
- The event resolves through the Stem’s biology and history

Let Continue is an intentional Consent choice.

---

# Field Entry Loop

Every resolved Becoming creates one Field Entry.

The player may:

- Inspect it
- Share its current visual representation
- Hold it
- Gift it
- List it
- Offer it in exchange
- Convert it to Premium while eligible
- Use it for Lineage Synthesis if Premium-illustrated and eligible

A stage transition embedded in a Becoming does not create a second Field Entry.

---

# Premium Conversion Loop

An active paid Bio-Lab Analyst may convert an eligible sketch Field Entry.

The process:

1. Select Create Canonical Illustration.
2. Review the Field Entry.
3. Review allowance balance.
4. Confirm one allowance reservation.
5. Lock the Field Entry.
6. Generate and validate the illustration.
7. Attach the permanent image.
8. Create the archive record.
9. Consume the allowance after success.
10. Unlock the card.

Failure restores the allowance and original card state.

---

# Lineage Loop

Lineage Synthesis requires:

- Paid or confirmed-grace Bio-Lab Analyst access
- An empty Hollow
- One owned Premium Field Entry
- An unused Genetic Entry
- Available transaction state

The player:

1. Selects a biome family.
2. Selects a sub-biome.
3. Selects Lineage Synthesis.
4. Selects an eligible Premium Field Entry.
5. Reviews irreversible consumption.
6. Confirms.
7. Receives a standard unresolved Stemling.

The resulting resemblance emerges later.

---

# Three-Stem Capacity Loop

The player may maintain up to three active Stems.

When all three Hollows are occupied:

- New Seed remains visible
- Lineage Synthesis remains visible
- Creation is blocked
- The player is told that care capacity is full

The game does not recommend which Stem to release.

The player must make the generational decision.

---

# Release Loop

Release is available when the Stem is biologically and transactionally eligible.

The player:

1. Selects Release.
2. Reviews a temporary farewell summary.
3. Reads the permanence disclosure.
4. Performs deliberate final confirmation.
5. Watches the Stem leave under its own movement.
6. Returns to an empty Hollow slot.

After release:

- The Stem disappears
- No biography remains
- No recall exists
- Existing Field Entries remain

---

# Adult Loop

Adults provide long-term play through:

- Sustainment
- Subtle behavioral observation
- Slower Becomings
- Additional Field Entries
- Recovery
- Lineage comparison
- Attachment
- Release decisions

Adult care must not reduce to merely preventing failure.

---

# Strain and Recovery Loop

When anatomy becomes difficult to sustain:

```text
Stable
↓
Strained
↓
Unstable
↓
Decaying
↓
Regression
```

Recovery may reverse:

```text
Strained → Stable
Unstable → Strained → Stable
Decaying → Unstable → Strained → Stable
```

Regression is irreversible for the released layer but preserves the Stem.

---

# Free Player Loop

Free Field Researchers retain indefinite access to:

- All three Hollows
- All 21 sub-biomes
- New Seed creation
- All lifecycle stages
- All Consent choices
- Adult care
- Sketch Field Entries
- Trading
- Gifting
- Permanent release
- Knowledge Base access

Free play does not end after onboarding.

---

# Bio-Lab Analyst Loop

Bio-Lab Analysts add:

- Automatic or manual canonical illustration
- Illustration allowance banking
- Premium-image access
- Illustrated Field Archive
- Full-resolution downloads
- Lineage Synthesis
- Personal Premium History

Premium features complement cultivation rather than replace it.

---

# Social Loop

## Gifting

Players may send an available Field Entry using a public account identifier.

The recipient must accept.

## Field Exchange

Players may:

- List one Field Entry
- Receive multiple one-for-one offers
- Accept or decline
- Complete one atomic final exchange

Players cannot inspect another player’s unlisted inventory.

---

# Offline Loop

Offline play is read-only for authoritative systems.

Players may:

- View cached state
- Read downloaded guidance
- Inspect cached sketches
- Adjust settings

Players may not:

- Change conditions
- Resolve biological events
- Transfer assets
- Generate images
- Create or release Stems

---

# Attention Management

The game must respect the player’s time.

## Safe waiting events

These wait for player input:

- Mutation Consent
- Final trade confirmation
- Gift acceptance
- Lineage Synthesis confirmation
- Release confirmation

## Background progression

The server may continue:

- Exposure to last confirmed conditions
- Comfort changes
- Recovery
- Pressure accumulation
- Foreshadowing

## Prohibited silent outcomes

The server may not silently:

- Choose Consent
- Consume a Genetic Entry
- Complete release
- Cross into irreversible Regression without a fair response opportunity
- Transfer ownership

---

# Notifications

Allowed notification categories:

- A Becoming is waiting
- Persistent strain is visible
- An intervention was paused
- Illustration completed
- Trade offer received
- Gift received
- Allowance cap notice
- Billing grace began

Notifications must not:

- Use panic language
- Reveal hidden biology
- Promise that stale transaction state remains available
- Pressure the player to spend allowances

---

# Failure Behavior

Every consequential loop must preserve authoritative state on failure.

## Hollow adjustment failure

- Restore last confirmed value
- Reconcile with server
- Do not invent a biological response

## Becoming interruption

- Enter safe pause
- Resume from authoritative state
- Do not punish brief connectivity loss

## Illustration failure

- Restore allowance
- Return card to sketch state
- Create no archive record

## Trade or gift failure

- Restore original ownership
- Release locks
- Avoid partial transfer

## Synthesis failure

- Preserve Genetic Entry
- Restore Field Entry
- Restore Hollow slot
- Create no partial Stemling

## Release failure

- Keep Stem active
- Keep Hollow occupied
- Show no completed farewell state

---

# Primary Gameplay Success Criteria

The core loop succeeds when:

- Players understand observation before optimization.
- Players distinguish immediate response from long-term development.
- The first Becoming occurs within the intended window.
- Consent feels consequential without becoming form selection.
- Field Entries feel meaningful before monetization.
- Adults remain worth retaining.
- Free players can continue indefinitely.
- Premium adds preservation and lineage rather than biological power.
- Three-Stem capacity creates meaningful generational decisions.
- Release feels like permanent departure rather than deletion.
- Failure never creates ambiguous ownership or biological state.
