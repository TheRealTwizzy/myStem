# Lore Canon

## Tonal premise

Stem presents an intimate biological world through warm-clinical observation. The surface is tender; the underlying implication is unsettling.

The player is never given a conventional exposition dump. World history should be inferred through terminology, archived notes, interface framing, environmental design, and occasional researcher observations.

## Origin

Stems originate from laboratory-modified human stem cells. They were engineered as endlessly adaptive living companions capable of surviving near human bodies and within changing human environments.

The Stemling is not a miniature version of a final adult. It is a stable, unresolved biological compromise: compact, warm-seeking, highly plastic, socially attentive, and safe enough to form attachment.

Its adult body emerges only through sustained environmental pressure.

## The core biological truth

A Stem does not have a hidden species waiting to emerge.

Its mature form is produced by:

- a fixed infant plan;
- a unique Biological Signature;
- accumulated Hollow history;
- prior anatomy;
- mutation operations;
- player consent;
- unresolved Mutation Debt;
- hidden lineage influence, when synthesized.

The adult is therefore a biological autobiography.

## Human anatomy remains visible

Every structure must originate from human anatomy or human-derived tissue.

An adult may achieve functions associated with flight, swimming, climbing, burrowing, filtering, anchoring, heat exchange, vibration sensing, or pressure resistance. Those functions must emerge through altered ribs, skin, fascia, digits, joints, lungs, palate, vessels, spine, muscle, or other human biological material.

## Awareness

Stems are non-verbal but eerily aware.

They may:

- track player interaction;
- wait for expected routines;
- recognize rhythms;
- alter posture when observed;
- anticipate environmental changes;
- remember care patterns;
- mirror non-verbal cadence.

They do not speak. No baseline system confirms human-level language or explicit sentience.

## Warmth

Warmth is the emotional and thematic root.

A Stemling seeks warmth as comfort. Later forms still seek warmth, but mature behavior may make the desire feel appetite-coded, possessive, or too attentive. The product implies this turn without depicting violence or predation.

## The wrong-note curve

### Stemling

- genuinely cute;
- compact and vulnerable;
- subtle prolonged gaze;
- slightly over-attentive response to touch or heat.

### Juvenile

- first structural asymmetry;
- unfamiliar movement;
- new environmental fixation;
- recognizable but altered human-derived tissue.

### Adolescent

- rapid integration problems;
- visible compensation;
- intensified awareness;
- a body that appears to be solving itself.

### Settled Form

- eerie-beautiful;
- fully individualized;
- capable, sustained, and structurally consequential;
- warmth-seeking demeanor may feel predatory without literal predation.

## Horror ceiling

Allowed:

- multiplied eyes derived from ocular tissue;
- fused or duplicated human-derived limbs;
- bone architecture beneath intact tissue;
- transparent or stretched skin;
- exposed-looking but fully sheathed vascular patterns;
- deep asymmetry;
- residual unused structures;
- abnormal stillness and gaze.

Forbidden:

- blood;
- open wounds;
- gore;
- traumatic amputation;
- explicit feeding on people or animals;
- sexualized biological imagery;
- torture;
- cruelty for shock.

## No fixed creature catalog

Named anatomical exemplars may exist in internal writing, historical notes, or creative references. They are not species and must not become a player-facing collection checklist.

Undiscovered variants always exist.

## Player role

The player is addressed as “you” and may be described as a Field Researcher. The game does not decide whether the player is caretaker, observer, companion, scientist, or custodian.

That ambiguity is intentional.

## Release

Release is permanent. The Stem leaves all player-facing systems. There is no later sighting, tracking record, return event, or recall.

Existing Field Entries remain because they are records of observed development, not ownership of the living Stem.

## Knowledge Base

The Knowledge Base may contain:

- necessary world context;
- gameplay guidance;
- researcher notes;
- biological terminology;
- archive and entitlement explanations.

It must not reveal:

- exact mutation recipes;
- hidden lineage traits;
- Mutation Debt values;
- optimal settings;
- guaranteed outcomes.
