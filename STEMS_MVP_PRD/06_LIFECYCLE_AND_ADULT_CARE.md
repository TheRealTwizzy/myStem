# Lifecycle and Adult Care

## Lifecycle stages

1. **Stemling**
2. **Juvenile**
3. **Adolescent**
4. **Settled Form**

The Choosing is a unique event within late Juvenile or early Adolescent development, not a separate permanent stage.

## Fixed pacing targets

- First Becoming: first active day, usually after two or three meaningful sessions.
- The Choosing: normally Becoming 3 or 4.
- Settled Form: normally after 4 to 6 total Becomings.
- Adult Becomings: slower, more structurally constrained, and increasingly shaped by sustainment.

Premium status and lineage do not alter pacing.

## Stemling

The Stemling is structurally unresolved.

Goals:

- establish attachment;
- teach observation;
- teach Hollow controls;
- create early cause-and-effect confidence;
- avoid visible specialization.

No permanent mutation should contradict the fixed spawn plan before the first Becoming resolves.

## Juvenile

The Juvenile establishes the first durable body-plan changes.

Expected characteristics:

- one or more clear anatomical changes;
- first persistent sustainment demands;
- visible residual infant anatomy;
- increased individuality;
- no fixed final-form commitment yet.

## Adolescent

Adolescence is the primary structural crisis.

Expected characteristics:

- exaggeration of earlier structures;
- compensatory or support growth;
- increased behavioral specificity;
- higher integration burden;
- The Choosing if not already resolved.

## The Choosing

The Choosing:

- uses the normal three Consent responses;
- occurs once;
- commits a dominant adaptive direction;
- does not present a form-selection menu;
- does not end later mutation;
- constrains future anatomy to build on existing structures;
- cannot be undone by regression, release, or synthesis.

## Settled Form

Settled Form is adulthood.

Adults continue to:

- express individualized behavior;
- require sustainment;
- experience slower Becomings;
- create additional Field Entries;
- enter strain, instability, or decay;
- recover;
- regress;
- be voluntarily released.

## Adult Sustainment Profile

Every settled adult has hidden requirements derived from:

- structural mass and distribution;
- respiratory and circulatory burden;
- tissue type;
- sensory specialization;
- locomotion method;
- temperature exchange;
- moisture dependence;
- light sensitivity;
- sub-biome-specific adaptations;
- compensatory dependencies.

The player receives broad observations, never exact thresholds.

## Biological condition states

### Stable

Current conditions adequately sustain existing anatomy.

### Strained

Mismatch is meaningful but recoverable. Biofeedback indicates the affected systems.

### Unstable

Multiple dependencies are failing or one critical dependency remains unsupported. Release is blocked.

### Decaying

The body is unable to sustain a mutation layer. Recovery is still possible before regression. Release is blocked.

### Regressed / Recovery

The least sustainable mutation layer has been released. The remaining body enters immediate recovery. Release is temporarily blocked.

## Regression

Regression is the ordinary-care failure floor.

Selection evaluates:

- sustainment burden;
- environmental mismatch;
- structural integration;
- dependency risk;
- post-removal viability;
- improvement to the remaining body.

Regression:

- removes one mutation layer;
- prefers the least sustainable and often newest viable layer;
- does not undo The Choosing;
- does not revert stage;
- does not create a Field Entry;
- produces an easier Sustainment Profile;
- cannot happen silently offline;
- never resembles traumatic amputation.

Visual treatment should suggest resorption, settling, folding, withdrawal, or gradual structural release.

## Recovery

Recovery requires:

- a valid post-regression anatomy graph;
- stable environmental support;
- visible improvement;
- no immediate second regression cascade;
- a protected recovery window.

## Permanent death

Ordinary cultivation cannot permanently kill a Stem in the MVP.

Any future narrative exception would require a separate product decision, rating review, data migration plan, and explicit exclusion from routine care.

## Release eligibility

Release is blocked during:

- Pending Consent;
- active Stabilization;
- active Suppression;
- Instability;
- Decay;
- immediate post-Regression recovery.

After confirmation:

- the Stem disappears from all player-facing interfaces;
- the Hollow becomes empty;
- no living history, sighting, or recall remains;
- existing Field Entries remain.
